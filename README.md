# Loci

A memory palace for studying **CAS exams** — each exam becomes its own estate, organized into domain wings and task rooms, built on the [method of loci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Method_of_loci).

---

## Vision

CAS Exam 6 is dense. Regulation, statutory accounting, solvency frameworks, professional responsibilities — hundreds of facts, formulas, and relationships that need to stick. Flashcards and outlines help, but they don't give you a *place* to put the material.

**Loci** turns each exam into an explorable estate. You choose an exam estate, move through domain wings, enter curated task rooms, and build mental routes through the syllabus. The goal isn't just to read the material — it's to *inhabit* it.

## The Method of Loci

The method of loci (memory palace technique) works by anchoring information to spatial locations you can mentally revisit. You imagine walking through a familiar building, placing vivid images at specific spots along the route, and later recall the information by retracing your steps.

Loci applies this to actuarial study:

- **Estates** hold full exam tracks — one estate per exam.
- **Wings** hold syllabus domains — regulation, financial reporting, professional responsibilities, reinsurance.
- **Rooms** within each wing hold the tasks for that domain.
- **Routes** connect related concepts across the estate.
- **Landmarks** make abstract material concrete and memorable.
- **Revisits** strengthen recall the same way walking a path again makes it familiar.

## Experience

**Exam estates + domain wings + task rooms.**

| Layer | Role |
|---|---|
| **Exam estate** | Each exam is its own estate — a top-level memory palace for that syllabus |
| **2D estate map** | Within an estate, navigate Domains A–D — see progress, choose a wing, unlock new areas |
| **Task rooms** | Each domain is a wing of the estate; within that wing, task rooms are full-screen illustrated scenes with hotspots, puzzles, and progression tied to exam concepts |

Inside each room, interaction is **point-and-click adventure** style: curated landmarks (the red binder, the wall chart, the sealed filing cabinet). You recall *where* in the scene an idea lived — that's method of loci, without 3D authoring.

**Not** Gather Town / tilemap avatars: those optimize for social presence, not spatial memory. Loci rooms are intentional dioramas where every object can carry exam content.

Each estate should feel worth exploring — mysterious, structured, and progressively revealing — not like a flat index of readings.

## Inspirations

Loci draws from two places. The **interaction model** comes first; **tone and structure** second.

**Primary — escape rooms & point-and-click adventures**

Hotspots, examine-and-unlock flow, puzzles that gate progress. The player discovers exam content by investigating a scene, not by reading a wall of text. This is the core loop.

**Secondary — [*Blue Prince*](https://www.blueprincegame.com/)**

Borrowed for mood and layout, not mechanics: an explorable manor, a map that structures your path, secrets hidden in chambers, progressive discovery. Loci is not first-person 3D and does not use room drafting — but it shares the feeling of an estate worth exploring.
